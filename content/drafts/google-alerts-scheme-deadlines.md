# How to Set Up Google Alerts So You Never Miss a Farm Scheme Deadline Again

Every year, farmers miss scheme deadlines. Not because they don't care — because they're busy, the dates change, and the notification from DAFM arrives when they're in the middle of something else.

Google Alerts is free, takes five minutes to set up, and sends you an email whenever something is published online about the topics you care about. You don't need to download anything. You don't need an app. You need a Gmail address and five minutes.

Here's exactly what to set up.

---

## What Google Alerts does

Google monitors the web for new content matching keywords you choose. When it finds something — a news article, a government update, a blog post — it emails you.

It's not AI in the flashy sense. It's a simple, free tool that's been around for years. And it works.

---

## Step 1: Go to google.com/alerts

Open your phone or laptop browser and go to [google.com/alerts](https://www.google.com/alerts). Sign in with your Google account if you're not already.

---

## Step 2: Create alerts for the schemes you're in

Type each of these into the search box and click "Create Alert":

**For BISS:**
> "BISS" Ireland deadline 2026

**For TAMS:**
> "TAMS III" Ireland application

**For ACRES:**
> "ACRES" Ireland scheme deadline

**For the Suckler Carbon Efficiency Programme:**
> "SCEP" Ireland scheme OR "Suckler Carbon Efficiency"

**For general farm scheme news:**
> Ireland farm scheme deadline 2026

You can add as many as you like. Each one generates a separate email when Google finds new results.

---

## Step 3: Adjust the settings

For each alert, click "Show options" and set:

- **How often:** Once a day (keeps your inbox manageable)
- **Sources:** Automatic (catches news, government sites, and blogs)
- **Language:** English
- **Region:** Ireland
- **How many:** Only the best results
- **Deliver to:** Your email address

Daily is the right frequency. Weekly means you might get a Monday email about a Friday deadline.

---

## Step 4: Add your own farm-specific alerts

If you're in a specific programme, add targeted alerts:

- Organic farming scheme Ireland
- Young farmer scheme Ireland
- Nitrates derogation Ireland 2026
- DAFM inspection Ireland

The more specific the alert, the less noise you'll get.

---

## What you'll actually receive

Within a day or two, you'll start getting emails like:

- "DAFM confirms BISS application window opens 15 April"
- "TAMS III Tranche 24 deadline extended to 30 June"
- "Teagasc urges farmers to check ACRES compliance before September"

You'll see it in your inbox instead of hearing about it at the mart a week too late.

---

## One extra step: add a Google Calendar reminder

For the deadlines you already know about, open Google Calendar on your phone and add them manually:

- BISS closing date
- TAMS next tranche deadline
- ACRES annual submission date

Set a reminder for two weeks before each one. That gives you time to gather documents and talk to your advisor.

Between Google Alerts catching the news and Google Calendar reminding you of the dates, you're covered.

---

## The bottom line

Missing a scheme deadline doesn't mean you failed at farming. It means the system is poorly designed for people who are busy actually farming. Google Alerts is a free, quiet tool that does the watching for you.

Five minutes now. No missed deadlines later.
