## The Press Release

Keep it under 400 words. Lead with the public interest angle, not the technology.

**Suggested headline:** "New Irish platform helps farmers cut through AI noise — in plain English, for free"

**Body:** AI is changing farming faster than most farmers have time to track. FarmAI Ireland is a new independent platform giving Irish farmers practical, plain-English guides to the tools that actually matter on the farm — from BISS applications to cattle health monitoring. Every guide is free, cites public sources, and links to official advice.

**Quote line:** "AI is moving at a pace nobody can keep up with. FarmAI Ireland just reads everything and tells you what matters for your farm — in five minutes or less."

## Audience Acquisition Strategy

Three phases, in priority order. Don't jump to phase 3 before phase 1 is working.

### Phase 1: PR and Word of Mouth (Months 1–3)

This is your fastest and cheapest channel. One radio piece on Shannonside is worth 10,000 SEO impressions for this audience. Farmers talk — one good guide shared on a WhatsApp group reaches 30 people you'd never find through Google.

- Send press release to all primary targets on launch day
- Identify 3–5 farmers or agri influencers (Agri Twitter, farming Facebook groups) and share first guides with them directly — not for review, just "thought you might find this useful"
- Post in Irish farming Facebook groups (Beef Farmers of Ireland, Irish Farmers, county-level groups) — not promotional, just share the guide that answers a question people are already asking
- LinkedIn post from your personal profile is optional but worth considering once you're ready to step forward

### Phase 2: SEO and YouTube (Months 2–6)

Content compounds. Every guide published is a permanent SEO asset. The key is that farmers are searching specific questions — "BISS application help", "best calving app", "ACRES scheme explained" — and we answer them better than anyone else.

- Prioritise FAQ-style headlines: "How do I...", "What is...", "Does AI help with..."
- FAQ schema markup on the homepage and long-form guides — gets you featured snippets
- Internal linking: every guide links to 2–3 related guides — keeps readers on site
- YouTube: start at Month 3 once content rhythm is established. Each guide becomes a 3–5 minute video. ElevenLabs voiceover + stock Irish farm footage. YouTube is also a search engine.

### Phase 3: Newsletter Growth (Ongoing)

The email list is the most valuable asset you're building. 500 engaged farmers who open every newsletter is worth more than 5,000 passive page views. Protect it.

- Sign-up incentive: free BISS application checklist PDF — builds list faster than "sign up for updates"
- Exit-intent popup on article pages after 60 seconds of reading time
- Every guide ends with a newsletter CTA — "If this was useful, get the monthly roundup"
- Open rate target: 40%+ (industry average is 22%). If it drops below 35%, pause and review content quality before sending more.
