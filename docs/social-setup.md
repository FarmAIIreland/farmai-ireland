## Platform Notes

### X (Twitter)
- Primary use: share articles, engage with agri journalists, tag Teagasc when citing them
- Post frequency: each new article + occasional one-liners
- Tone: same as site — dry, honest, plain English
- No threads at launch — save the effort

### Facebook Page
- Primary use: reach farmers aged 35–60 who aren't on X
- Share each article with a one-line hook — not the headline verbatim
- Consider boosting posts to Irish farming interests for €5–10 once the page has 50+ likes
- Join Irish farming groups and share relevant articles as genuine answers to questions being asked (not promotional)

### LinkedIn Page
- Primary use: B2B visibility — sponsors, media, co-ops, industry contacts
- Tone: slightly more formal than the site, but still plain English
- Share articles framed for the agri-business reader, not just the farmer

### YouTube
- Start posting at Month 3 when video pipeline is established (ElevenLabs + Pictory)
- Subscribe target: 1,000 for YouTube Partner Programme
- Add channel description: "Plain English AI guides for Irish farmers. New video every week. All guides also at farmai.ie"

### Instagram
- Lowest priority — repurpose existing content rather than creating native posts
- Use Canva to reformat article thumbnails as square/portrait posts
- Stories: share articles via link sticker once you have 200+ followers

### TikTok — Deferred to Month 6
- Revisit at Month 6 once YouTube is established
- If pursuing TikTok: repurpose YouTube Shorts (vertical crop) rather than native creation
- Handle to register when ready: @farmaiiireland (three i's — FarmAI + Ireland)
